How to Play

1

The Setup

One player thinks of a secret object and gives clues to the other players to help them guess what the secret object is.

2

The Clues

Each clue object must share at least one attribute with the secret object. The attributes are shape, size, color, and category.

3

Take a Guess

After each clue, don't be afraid to guess! Each new clue object must share a new attribute with the secret object.

Example Game

Secret object: pencil
Clue 1: school bus - A pencil and a school bus share the same color. However, the guessers don't know what attribute a school bus shares with the secret object. From their perspective, the secret object could be a loaf of bread (shape), an elephant (size), a limousine (category).

Clue 2: piece of paper - A pencil and a piece of paper are both in the same category. From the guesser's perspective, there are lots of different combinations of what the secret object could be. It could be a white vehicle. Or, it could be a massive blanket (same size as a school bus and same shape as a piece of paper).

Clue 3: knife - A pencil and a knife are the same size. There are so many possibilities for the secret object. It could be a yellow, rectangular kitchen utensil!

Clue 4: pool noodle - A pencil and a pool noodle are the same shape. Notice that each clue object shared a different attribute with the secret object.

Remember

Be creative! There's flexibility in how attributes match. A potato and a baseball might be similar sizes to one person but different to another. The fun is in thinking creatively!
